## Overview

Edge AI is transforming robotics by enabling machines to process data locally — making decisions in milliseconds rather than waiting for cloud round-trips. This case study explores how edge intelligence in robotics has evolved across four stages, and what it means for building production-ready autonomous systems.

## The Four Stages of Edge Intelligence in Robotics

### Stage 1: Rule-Based Systems

Simple sensors, predictable inputs, and predefined actions in controlled environments. Think of a pick-and-place robot on a factory floor responding to objects on a conveyor belt using deterministic decision trees.

### Stage 2: Computer Vision on the Edge

With more processing power, robots could handle richer inputs like RGB cameras. They could detect and locate multiple objects, but the environment still needed to be highly controlled.

### Stage 3: Machine Learning on the Edge

Robots became better at handling variation in lighting, background, object type, and quality. The system could not only detect an object, but classify it, assess its quality, and take the right action. But it still only understood what it had been trained to understand.

### Stage 4: LLMs and Vision-Language Models on the Edge

This is where things get really interesting. We can now begin to bring reasoning, contextual understanding, and general knowledge closer to the robot itself. In a conveyor belt scenario, the system could reason: "Stop the belt if there is a safety hazard." That hazard could be a worker standing too close, products being stacked incorrectly, or an unexpected object entering the scene.

## Key Takeaways

- Edge AI eliminates cloud latency and connectivity dependencies for robotic systems
- The progression from rule-based to LLM-powered edge intelligence represents a fundamental shift in robot capability
- Production-ready edge AI requires careful architecture decisions around model size, power budget, and hardware selection
- Hybrid architectures — combining small edge models with occasional cloud reasoning — often provide the best balance

> "This is a major shift. We're moving from robots that follow instructions to robots that understand context."
